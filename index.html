Welcome to Miami!

A city of diversity, luxury, sunshine, all night parties, and coastline. Whilst groups and people of all ages frequently visit for a beach getaway or party, over 6 million people call this metropolitan area home.

Miami is a particularly vulnerable coastal region to sea level rise due to its 17 miles of beach access, low lying topography, and most of the state sitting on a bed of porous limestone, making most traditional climate change relief methodologies ineffective. Let’s take a look at the varying sea levels and climate risks Miami has endured throughout the 2000s, and will continue to endure for decades to come.
